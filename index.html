Welcome to Resistance Zero

Resistance Zero transforms procrastination into a navigation tool. Instead of fighting resistance, we use it as guidance - scanning tasks until some feel effortless, then acting on those first.

The 5-Step Process:

  1. List Building – Capture everything into one flat list
  2. Scanning – Pass through quickly and dot zero-resistance tasks
  3. Action – Take small steps on dotted items
  4. Maintenance – Clear completed and irrelevant tasks
  5. Reflection – Notice progress and patterns
Step 1 of 5

Step 1: List Building

Empty your head into one flat list. Capture everything that isn't tied to a calendar or specific time. Don't worry about organization, priorities, or categories - just get it all out.

What to do:

  • Add tasks quickly at any level (project, step, or meta-task)
  • Keep the list flat and uncategorized
  • Include everything that's on your mind
  • Don't worry about perfect wording

    Step 2: Scanning

    Move through your list in one smooth pass. Dot anything that feels effortless - these will lead the next stage. Trust your gut feeling about what has zero resistance.

    What to do:

    • Choose a direction (forward or backward) and stick with it
    • Pass through quickly - don't overthink
    • Dot tasks that feel effortless or exciting
    • Skip anything that creates resistance
    • Complete the full scan before moving on

    Step 3: Action

    Follow the dots you created. Small moves count - each touch lowers resistance and builds momentum. Focus on progress, not perfection.

    What to do:

    • Pick a dotted task and take a light, obvious next step
    • Work "little and often" - even 2 minutes counts
    • Log time if useful for productivity or billing
    • Re-enter incomplete tasks - they'll move to the end of the list
    • Celebrate small wins

    Step 4: Maintenance

    Clear space so only what's alive stays in view. This is where clutter melts away and clarity emerges. Trust your intuition about what no longer belongs.

    What to do:

    • Scan for tasks that are done, irrelevant, or waiting on others
    • Delete tasks that "stand out" as no longer relevant
    • Archive completed work for reflection
    • Re-enter recurring tasks when it's time for another pass
    • Keep the list clean and honest

    Maintenance

    Delete what no longer belongs. Archived tasks stay visible in Reflection.

    Step 5: Reflection

    Take a breath and notice what shifted. Reflection locks in momentum and points you toward the next cycle. This is where you celebrate progress and learn from patterns.

    What to do:

    • Celebrate completed tasks and time logged
    • Look for patterns in what moved easily vs. what resisted
    • Notice how resistance changed over multiple scans
    • Note insights to carry forward to the next cycle
    • Acknowledge the clumping effect of re-entered tasks
